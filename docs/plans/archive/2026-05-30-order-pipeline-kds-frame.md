# Order Pipeline + KDS Production-Room Display — Frame Spec

**Status:** FORGE Phase 1 (Frame) — Gate 1 spec, awaiting Hakan sign-off
**Created:** 2026-05-30
**Plan size:** XL (multi-week, multiple sub-branches)
**Type:** New app feature, replaces a 100+ customer workflow currently running on WhatsApp

## Goal

Replace the "WhatsApp + screenshot + retype into BarcodeX" order-capture workflow with a single digital flow inside mfsops.com, paired with a kitchen-display-system style touchscreen in the production room. Weights and invoicing remain manual in BarcodeX for now (Fresho transition is a separate, future project). The system replaces the *capture, visibility, and lock-out* parts of the workflow only.

## What problem is being solved

Today's flow:

1. Sales rep types order into the "meat orders" WhatsApp group
2. Office reads WhatsApp, types order into BarcodeX, prints A4 picking list
3. Butcher cuts, weighs, prints scale-ticket labels, sticks them onto the picking list, hands paper back to office
4. Office reads weights off the stuck-on labels, finalises invoice in BarcodeX

Specific failure modes this flow produces:
- Orders missed in long WhatsApp threads
- Office retyping = double-entry + transcription errors
- No audit trail of who placed which order when
- No live visibility for warehouse / office / Hakan / Ege without scrolling WhatsApp
- Sales reps can't see other reps' orders for the same customer (overlap risk)
- Late edits handled via "phone the office" — no system-of-record

## What is NOT in scope

Listing these explicitly so we don't scope-creep mid-build:

- **Weight capture inside mfsops.** Scale-ticket labels stay as-is. Office still keys weights into BarcodeX for invoicing.
- **Invoicing inside mfsops.** BarcodeX (and later Fresho) remains the invoice system. mfsops never sees a price.
- **Customer-facing portal.** Sales reps place orders for customers; customers don't self-serve.
- **Customer notifications.** WhatsApp confirmation pings to customers are a possible Phase 2 — not at launch.
- **Delivery routing / driver assignment.** Out of scope; lives elsewhere in mfsops or in future work.
- **Inventory deduction.** Cutting 13.67kg of lamb shoulder doesn't update an inventory figure in mfsops. Future work.

## Locked decisions (from grilling)

### Sales-side flow (rep-facing, mfsops.com on phone or laptop)

1. Rep selects a customer from the existing mfsops customers list
2. Rep adds one or more line items. For each line:
   - Either pick a catalogued product (from the existing mfsops products table — single source of truth), OR
   - Add an **ad-hoc item** = free-text description + quantity + unit (switcher: kg or unit). No product code. No price field.
   - Each line has its own **per-line notes** text field (e.g. "tied", "extra trim", "20g portions")
3. Order also has an **order-level notes** text field (e.g. "deliver before 11am")
4. Order has a **delivery date** (mandatory) and optional **delivery time / slot** (still to grill in Gate 2 — see open question O1)
5. Rep confirms the order. Generated reference: **`MFS-YYYY-NNNN`** format (sequential, generated by mfsops, independent of BarcodeX SO-numbers)
6. After confirm, the rep can keep editing the order **until the office prints the picking sheet** — at which point the order is locked from the rep

### Office-side flow (mfsops.com dashboard, typically iPad or laptop)

7. Office arrives in the morning, sees all open orders for the day on a dashboard
8. Office reviews each order, taps **Print picking list** — A4 picking sheet prints
9. **The print action locks the order from sales-rep edits.** Inline UI warning next to the Print button: *"Printing will lock this order from sales edits"*
10. If a late amendment is needed (e.g. customer phones after print): **office is the only role that can edit** the locked order. After editing, a reprint is required. The system does not try to enforce paper-handling discipline — that's an office process problem.
11. After butchery completes, office reads weights off the scale-ticket labels and keys them into BarcodeX for invoicing (unchanged from today)

### Production room flow (KDS — dedicated touchscreen in process room)

12. Every order appears as a card on the KDS screen, ordered by delivery date then by print order
13. Each card shows: customer, order reference, delivery date, order-level notes, **and each line item as its own row inside the card**
14. **Each line item has its own Done tap** — butcher works through the picking sheet, taps the corresponding KDS line as each line is weighed and labelled
15. When all lines on a card are done, the card transitions to **completed** state
16. If the office amends a locked order, **the affected card flashes orange** on the KDS — visual signal that the butcher needs to re-check the paper sheet against the updated card
17. Butchers do not log into mfsops on a phone or tablet — KDS is their only screen for this workflow

### Visibility / roles

| Role | Sees what |
|---|---|
| Sales reps | All orders (own + others) — overlap visibility |
| Office | All orders, all states |
| Warehouse | All orders (for goods-out, pallet prep, vehicle loading) |
| Butchers | KDS only — no mfsops login for this workflow |
| Hakan / Ege | All orders + admin functions (analytics is Phase 2) |
| Drivers | Out of scope for MVP |

Anyone with order visibility can press **Print picking list**. Print always locks the order. Print button always shows the inline warning.

### Order states (three only)

1. **`placed`** — sales has confirmed, office hasn't printed yet, sales can still edit, KDS shows the card
2. **`printed`** — office has printed the picking sheet, sales is locked out, only office can edit (which triggers reprint + orange flash on KDS)
3. **`completed`** — all line items on the KDS have been tapped done, card moves out of the active queue

No "acknowledged" state. No "in production" state — printed and in-production are the same thing because butchers cut continuously.

### Cutover strategy (parallel running with WhatsApp)

Replacing the WhatsApp group cold-turkey is dangerous. Proposed phased cutover (subject to Hakan's call at sign-off):

1. **Weeks 1–2:** Sales reps post to WhatsApp AND enter into mfsops. Butchers work off WhatsApp screenshot printout as today. Production KDS shows the orders so butchers get familiar with the screen.
2. **Weeks 3–4:** Sales reps enter into mfsops ONLY. Office prints from mfsops. WhatsApp group becomes fallback only — used if mfsops is down.
3. **Week 5+:** WhatsApp meat-orders group is retired.

Total: ~4 weeks parallel running before WhatsApp shutdown.

Open at Gate 1 — to confirm in Gate 2 (planner) before building. Options:

| Option | Cost | Ruggedness | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Wall-mounted 21–24" touchscreen + small mini-PC** | ~£400–£600 | Best — keyboard not needed, plenty of viewing distance | Most "real KDS" feel. Mini-PC runs Chrome kiosk-mode on mfsops.com/kds |
| **B — Spare iPad on a stand** | ~£300–£500 | OK — IPX-rated case essential | Familiar hardware, screen is small for cross-room visibility |
| **C — Another Sunmi V3-class Android device** | ~£200 | Good | Tiny screen — bad for cross-room visibility, only suits one-butcher operation |
| **D — Repurposed laptop** | £0–£200 | Worst — keyboard near meat, slip hazard | Cheapest, ugliest, not recommended |

**Recommendation pending Hakan confirm:** Option A. Process room is wet/cold, screen needs to be visible from the cutting bench, mini-PC behind glass or in IP-rated enclosure. Provisional spec: 24" touchscreen monitor + Beelink-class mini-PC running Chromium kiosk mode pointed at `mfsops.com/kds`.

This is the only hardware to procure for this feature. No printer changes (existing label printers + A4 office printer cover everything).

## Picking sheet layout

Based on the existing BarcodeX picking list (Hakan provided photo on 30 May 2026), the printed A4 sheet must reproduce these columns and fields:

**Header:**
- Customer name (line 1, large)
- Customer address block (street, town, postcode) — left
- Account Code, Sales Rep initials, Order No (`MFS-YYYY-NNNN`), Date — right
- Delivery date if different from order date

**Body (table):**
| Code | UOM | Qty | Description | Shelf No | Unit Size | Pack |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

For ad-hoc items: Code column is blank, Description has the free-text, Qty + UOM populated, other columns empty.

**Per-line notes:** printed below the line in a smaller font, italic — so handwriting space is preserved on the sheet but the rep's note prints automatically (replaces the pen scribbles "tied", "20 shaks" etc. seen on today's sheet).

**Order-level notes:** printed at the foot of the body table, before footer.

**Footer:**
- "Printed at [timestamp] by [user]"
- "No. of pallets: _____" (handwritten)
- Order reference repeated as a barcode bottom-right (helps office trace back when scale tickets are stuck on)

Existing label printing infrastructure (`lib/printing/`, `api/labels/`) likely needs a new `type=picking-list` template. To confirm in Gate 2.

## Open questions parked for Gate 2 (planner phase)

These are answered during planning, not now — surfacing them here so they don't get lost:

- **O1. Delivery date — and what about delivery time/slot?** Today's WhatsApp orders may include "before 11am" in the message body. Should that be a structured field or stay as a free-text order-level note?
- **O2. The mfsops products table — is it actually populated and current?** Pre-Gate 2 work: query `products` table in Supabase, compare against the BarcodeX-style picking-list catalogue, identify gaps. If a gap exists, schedule a one-time import.
- **O3. Order edits after print — what's the audit trail?** Office edits a locked order. Do we want a per-order audit log (who edited, when, what changed) for dispute traceability?
- **O4. Reprints — version number on the picking sheet?** If a sheet is reprinted, should it show "v2" or a re-print timestamp so the butcher knows which physical sheet is current?
- **O5. KDS card auto-archive.** When `completed`, does the card disappear immediately, fade out, or stay greyed-out at the bottom until office archives? (My instinct: stay visible until end-of-day, then auto-archive — but Hakan to confirm.)
- **O6. Realtime transport choice.** Supabase realtime channels vs Postgres LISTEN/NOTIFY vs polling. Supabase realtime is the default — to verify Phase 2 hasn't hit rate limits since we last looked.
- **O7. RLS (Row-Level Security) — who can do what.** Drafted in Gate 2 against the visibility table above. Butchers don't log in, sales reps see everything, office can edit locked orders — these are policy decisions, not feature decisions.
