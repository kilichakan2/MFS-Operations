## Executive summary

F-08 is the first full route-family rewrite onto the hexagonal shape: the five Orders/KDS
endpoints now go route → zod validation → OrdersService → port, with legacy wire shapes
pinned byte-for-byte by DTO key-set tests. The headline behaviour addition is idempotent
order placement: a client-supplied Idempotency-Key header makes "place order" safe to retry
(double-tap, flaky wifi) — replay returns the original order, a same-instant race is
arbitrated by the table's PRIMARY KEY with loser-cleanup, a cross-user key reuse is refused
with 409 and zero data leakage, and keys self-expire after 24h with opportunistic reclaim.

In plain English: tapping "place order" twice — even in the same instant, even on bad
wifi — can no longer create two orders, and nobody else can use your retry ticket to see
your order.
